Why We Build On Google Sheets (Not Replace It)

Our philosophy: meet clients where they are. Google Sheets isn't a limitation — it's a powerful automation platform when enhanced properly.

The Migration Trap

There's a pattern in automation consulting that drives me crazy:

Client has a problem with their Google Sheets workflow. Consultant diagnoses it, nods sagely, and says: "You've outgrown Sheets. You need [expensive enterprise platform]. Here's a quote for £25,000."

That's not consulting. That's product-first thinking dressed up as advice.

Here's the truth: 90% of businesses that think they've "outgrown" Google Sheets haven't outgrown the platform — they've outgrown their current implementation.

And fixing the implementation is orders of magnitude cheaper, faster, and less disruptive than migrating to a new system.

What Google Sheets Actually Is

Most people see Google Sheets as "a spreadsheet program." That's underselling it by about 90%.

Google Sheets is:

  • A collaborative database with built-in access controls
  • An automation platform (Google Apps Script is JavaScript with API access to the entire Google Workspace)
  • A UI layer that everyone already knows how to use
  • A reporting engine with native visualization tools
  • A webhook endpoint that can trigger workflows in n8n, Zapier, Make

When you see it this way, suddenly the question changes from "Should we migrate off Sheets?" to "How do we use Sheets as the foundation it's meant to be?"

The Cost of Migration Nobody Talks About

Let's say you migrate from Google Sheets to a £15,000 financial management platform. Here's what you're actually paying:

Direct costs:

  • £15,000 - £50,000: Platform license (first year)
  • £10,000 - £30,000: Implementation / consulting
  • £5,000 - £15,000: Training

Hidden costs:

  • 3-6 months: Migration timeline (your team is operating half-blind during this)
  • 2-3 months: Learning curve after launch (productivity dip while everyone figures out the new system)
  • Ongoing: Vendor lock-in (switching costs only increase over time)
  • Ongoing: You now depend on a vendor's roadmap, not your own needs

Total: £30,000 - £95,000 in year one, plus 6-9 months of disruption.

Alternative: £1,200 - £3,000 to enhance your existing Google Sheets with automation, validation, and structured workflows. Deployed in 1-3 weeks. Zero retraining.

The ROI math isn't subtle.

What "Enhancement" Looks Like

When we say "enhance," here's what we mean:

1. Apps Script automation

  • Replace volatile formulas with triggered functions (faster, more reliable)
  • Auto-archive old data to keep working datasets lean
  • Custom functions for complex business logic
  • Scheduled reports that generate themselves

2. Validation and protection

  • Lock critical formulas and calculation cells
  • Smart validation that rejects bad data at entry
  • Audit trails that log every change (who, what, when)
  • Approval workflows for sensitive edits

3. External integrations

  • n8n workflows that sync Sheets with your CRM, accounting system, etc.
  • Slack/Teams notifications for key events
  • API connections to external data sources
  • Webhook triggers for real-time updates

Result: Your Google Sheet feels like an expensive enterprise system — instant updates, structured workflows, full audit trails — but it's still the same tool your team already knows.

When We Do Recommend Migration

We're not dogmatic. There are real limits to Google Sheets, and we'll tell you when you've hit them:

Migrate when:

  • Row-level security is mandatory: You need each user to only see their own data, with no workaround (e.g., salespeople should never see each other's pipelines)
  • You're consistently hitting 10 million cells even with optimization
  • Real-time transactional logic is required: Like inventory management with stock locking across multiple warehouses
  • Compliance demands immutable audit trails that Google Sheets' version history can't provide

But for most SMBs, solopreneurs, and agencies? You're nowhere near these limits. You're losing time to manual processes, not platform constraints.

Real Example: From "Needs Migration" to £1,200 Fix

Client: 8-person marketing agency

Problem: Client pipeline spreadsheet was "too slow, too error-prone, collaboration is chaotic."

What other consultants recommended: Migrate to a project management platform (£12k/year) or custom database (£25k build).

What we did:

  • Set up n8n workflow: Sheets → HubSpot sync (auto-update CRM)

Cost: £1,200 one-time

Timeline: 2 weeks

Retraining required: Zero (same interface, better guardrails)

Result: Load time dropped from 30 seconds to 3. Formula errors eliminated. Collaboration conflicts resolved. Time saved: 6 hours/week.

They've scaled from 8 to 14 people since then. Still using the same enhanced Sheets system.

Our Philosophy: Tool-Agnostic, Outcome-Focused

We're not "Google Sheets evangelists." We're problem solvers who believe in meeting clients where they are.

If you're already using Google Sheets: We'll make it work like a £50k system.

If you're using Excel: We'll enhance that instead.

If you're using Airtable, Notion, or a custom database: We'll automate the manual parts, not rip it out.

If you genuinely need to migrate: We'll tell you, help you pick the right platform, and build the migration workflow.

But 9 times out of 10? The problem isn't the tool. It's the lack of automation, validation, and structure.

Fix those, and you'll get years of value out of the system you already have — with a fraction of the cost and disruption of migration.

The Bottom Line

We build on Google Sheets because:

  • It's already adopted — your team knows it
  • It's flexible — we can enhance it to do almost anything
  • It's cost-effective — £1,200 beats £30,000 every time
  • It's fast to deploy — weeks, not months
  • It's yours — no vendor lock-in, no recurring fees

This isn't about defending a tool. It's about respecting your time, budget, and workflow.

If Google Sheets (or Excel, or whatever you're using) can be enhanced to solve your problem, that's what we'll recommend.

No upsell. No vendor kickbacks. Just the most efficient path to getting your time back.
